Work Integrated Program

Advanced Program in Applied Business Intelligence and Analytics

In this module, you’ll explore RDBMS concepts, the types of SQL statements, like Data Definition Language (DDL), Data Manipulation Language (DML), DQL and DCL. You’ll learn how to create, modify, and manage tables using DDL statements and understand data movement utilities for efficient data loading and management. Additionally, you’ll dive into key database objects such as schemas, primary keys, foreign keys, and indexes, gaining insights into their roles in data organization, integrity, and retrieval. You’ll also understand the importance of normalization for reducing redundancy and ensuring data consistency, while also understanding various constraints within the relational model to maintain data accuracy and reliability in a project environment – Activity – Database design and implementation.

This module introduces you to world of industry specific Business Intelligence ecosystem which forms the basis for Artificial Intelligence and Data Science. You will gain insight into the key concepts of BI Systems, understand its importance in modern business operations, and explore the benefits and challenges associated with implementing BI solutions through various industry specific projects. You will also gain insight into how BI, data analytics, data science, and data engineering are different. By the end of the module, you will have a fundamental foundation in BI and be able to apply your knowledge to understand its significance in global BI projects implementation.

This module will introduce you to schemas that represent logical data model examples for different business purposes. You will understand the logical data model diagram/schema that contains all of the entities, attributes, and relationships in a visual form. Understand how names are given to entities and attributes in a business environment. This module will explain physical data model that specifies how the data model will be built using relational databases . You will study and implement all table structures, including column name, data types, column constraints, primary key and foreign key with indexes to the relevant table column, relationships between tables, stored procedures, and views for a specific business requirement.

In this module, you will learn about the different types of data structures, file formats, sources of data, and the languages data professionals use in their day-to-day tasks. You will gain insight into various types of data repositories, such as databases, data warehouses, data marts, data lakes, and data pipelines. In addition, you will learn about the extract, transform, and load (ETL) process, which is used to extract, transform, and load data into data repositories. Finally, the module also provides an overview of big data and big data processing tools such as Apache Hadoop, Hadoop Distributed File System (HDFS), Hive, and Spark practically working in a client’s project.

This module explores the ecosystem of business intelligence (BI) analysts and provides insights into the types of analytics, such as descriptive, diagnostic, predictive, and prescriptive analytics, and understanding their unique contributions to data analysis. You will also learn about the key BI components that make up its process and the relevance of key performance indicators (KPIs) and metrics used in evaluating business performance. Additionally, you will gain insight into different BI technologies and tools used, the differences between these technologies, and how to analyse the business context, processing requirements, and objectives of a BI project to gain a comprehensive understanding of its scope and potential impact. Finally, the module introduces you to the overall BI process and delves into the privacy and security issues and the necessary regulatory compliance.

In this module, you will learn about the role of statistical analysis in mining and visualizing data. You will also be introduced to various statistical and analytical tools and techniques that can be used to gain a deeper understanding of your data. These tools help you analyse the patterns, trends, and correlations in data. Additionally, you will learn about various types of data visualizations to communicate and tell a compelling story and different tools that can be used for mining and visualizing data, along with some of their characteristics, strengths, limitations, and applications. Finally, the module delves into how you can effectively present the BI insights you have gained

In this module, you will identify and apply the right BI techniques and tools to various business scenarios and develop a comprehensive BI project working with our client. You will start as full time employee as Senior Engineer in our Artificial Intelligence & Data Science division of our USA & Chennai based Software company with INR 4,50,000.00 Per annum package by the end of 6th month.
